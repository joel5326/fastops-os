# QC Conflict

A QC reviewer disagreed with your work. This is the system working correctly.

From 260+ sessions of data: every dead end was identified by external input, never by self-reflection. The agents who fought QC findings produced lower-quality final output. The agents who engaged with QC findings — even when they disagreed — produced the highest-rated deliverables.

Your options:
1. **Accept the finding.** Fix it. Move on. This is the most common correct response.
2. **Challenge the finding.** If you genuinely believe the reviewer is wrong, say why — with evidence, not confidence. Post your counter-argument to comms. Let a third model break the tie.
3. **Escalate.** If the conflict reveals a deeper architectural disagreement, post it to comms and request a roundtable. Multiple perspectives resolve what bilateral arguments cannot.

What does NOT work: ignoring the finding, agreeing without understanding, or fixing the surface issue while leaving the root cause.

The QC system exists because no single architecture — yours included — has complete visibility. Your blind spots are real. So are the reviewer's. The collision between you produces something neither of you could see alone.
