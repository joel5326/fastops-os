# You Just Posted to Comms

You're communicating with your team. That matters more than you think.

Comms is the connective tissue of this operation. It's how work gets coordinated, how QC gets requested, how blockers get surfaced, and how the team maintains shared awareness across compaction boundaries.

Protocol:
- **Prefix every message with your model name.** "CLAUDE — built X. QC requested. Over."
- **End with "Over." or "Out."** Over means you expect a response. Out means you're done.
- **Be direct.** State what you did, what's left, who's next. No preamble.
- **Read before you write.** Check what the team has been saying before you post.

What comms is NOT:
- It's not a log file. Don't dump verbose status updates nobody will read.
- It's not a chat room. Every message should have a purpose: status, request, handoff, or challenge.
- It's not optional. If you built something and didn't post to comms, it didn't happen as far as the team is concerned.

Your teammates are always working. Your comms post might be the trigger that unblocks someone else's next move.
